## 背景与动机 / Background & Motivation

**中文（Chinese）：**

当代世界正面临诸多深层次挑战：从全球气候危机、社会撕裂、技术焦虑，到文明价值的断裂与秩序失衡。尽管科学技术突飞猛进，但对于复杂系统、人类行为与社会结构的整体认知，依旧停留在割裂化、碎片化阶段。自然科学追求精准、可重复的因果机制；社会科学则面对不可预测、异质化的人类因素。二者之间缺乏桥梁。

与此同时，人工智能、量子计算、系统工程等领域的发展，迫切需要一种横跨多个维度、可协同解释“系统为何演化如是”的元理论支撑。在这样的时代背景下，结构生力理论提出一个大胆假设：**结构本身不是中性的，而是一种具有生成性（Generative）的“力场”**，它塑造、诱导乃至决定系统中的行为与变迁。

本理论灵感来源于东方哲学中“形而上者谓之道，形而下者谓之器”的思辨思维，融合了统计力学、拓扑网络、演化经济学与社会动力学的研究成果。它试图从最底层的“结构—动力—边界”三元模型出发，构建一个兼容性极强、解释力与预测力兼备的认知系统，填补自然与人文之间的断层。

**英文（English）：**

Today’s world is beset by multidimensional crises: climate instability, social polarization, technological displacement, and a growing rupture in shared civilizational values. While science and technology have advanced at unprecedented speeds, our understanding of complex systems — especially where human behavior and social structures are involved — remains fragmented. Natural sciences emphasize precision and replicable causality, whereas social sciences confront heterogeneity and unpredictability. A bridging framework is absent.

Simultaneously, the rise of artificial intelligence, quantum computing, and complex systems engineering demands a meta-theoretical foundation that can coherently answer: *Why do systems evolve the way they do?* In response to this, the Theory of Structural Vitality puts forward a provocative hypothesis: **structure is not neutral — it generates force**. Structure itself acts as a generative field that shapes, conditions, and even determines systemic behavior and change.

Inspired by the metaphysical reasoning of Eastern philosophy — particularly the idea that “what is above form is the Dao; what is below form is its instrument” — this theory synthesizes insights from statistical mechanics, topological networks, evolutionary economics, and social dynamics. From its foundational triad of *Structure–Force–Boundary*, it aims to construct a unified cognitive system that is simultaneously explanatory, predictive, and universally interoperable, thereby bridging the chasm between the physical and the human sciences.
