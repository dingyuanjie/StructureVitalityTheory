# I. 自然科学  
## 5. 地球科学

# Tectonic and Climate Systems as Structural Vital Force Networks

---

## 摘要 (Abstract)

**中文**：  
将板块构造、大气环流、洋流模式统一解释为地球系统中的能量螺旋传递过程，提出结构生力网络模型，用于描述地球系统能量分布及稳定性。该模型可以预测极端气候事件和地震的临界条件及时空分布，为地球科学研究提供新的理论工具。  

**English**:  
Plate tectonics, atmospheric circulation, and ocean current patterns are unified as spiral energy transfer processes within Earth's systems. We propose a Structural Vital Force network model to describe energy distribution and stability in Earth systems. This model can predict critical conditions and spatiotemporal distributions of extreme climate events and earthquakes, offering a novel theoretical tool for geoscience research.

---

## 验证思路 (Validation Approach)

**中文**：  
1. 历史数据回测：对比板块移动速率、地震发生记录与模型预测临界条件；  
2. 气候模拟对比：使用全球气候模型(GCM)分析极端气候事件出现频率与模型拟合度；  
3. 洋流与能量分布分析：通过观测数据与螺旋能量传递模型对比，验证洋流模式稳定性。  

**English**:  
1. Historical data back-testing: compare plate motion rates and earthquake records with predicted critical conditions.  
2. Climate simulation comparison: use Global Climate Models (GCM) to analyze frequency of extreme climate events versus model predictions.  
3. Ocean current and energy distribution analysis: validate spiral energy transfer model against observational data to assess ocean current stability.

## 理论公式示例 (Example Theoretical Formulas)

**中文**：  
地球系统螺旋能量传递函数：  
\[
E_{SVF}(x,t) = \sum_i f(r_i, \theta_i, \rho_i, t)
\]  
其中 \(r_i\) 表示能量传递距离，\(\theta_i\) 为局部螺旋角，\(\rho_i\) 为密度或能量密度。  

板块稳定性临界条件：  
\[
\sigma_{crit} = \sigma_0 + \alpha \cdot f(\theta, \rho)
\]  

**English**:  
Earth system spiral energy transfer function:  
\[
E_{SVF}(x,t) = \sum_i f(r_i, \theta_i, \rho_i, t)
\]  
where \(r_i\) is energy transfer distance, \(\theta_i\) is local spiral angle, and \(\rho_i\) is density or energy density.  

Plate stability critical condition:  
\[
\sigma_{crit} = \sigma_0 + \alpha \cdot f(\theta, \rho)
\]

## 研究展望 (Research Outlook)

**中文**：  
- 将结构生力网络与高精度地球观测数据结合，提升地震与极端气候预测精度；  
- 探索大气环流、洋流与板块运动之间的跨尺度耦合机制；  
- 提供统一的地球系统动力学解释框架，为地球科学与环境管理提供理论支撑。  

**English**:  
- Integrate Structural Vital Force networks with high-precision Earth observation data to improve earthquake and extreme climate prediction.  
- Explore cross-scale coupling mechanisms between atmospheric circulation, ocean currents, and plate tectonics.  
- Provide a unified framework for Earth system dynamics, offering theoretical support for geoscience and environmental management.
