# III. 人文与社会科学  
## 11. 政治学与制度设计

# Structural Vital Balance in Multi-Polar Governance

---

## 摘要 (Abstract)

**中文**：  
将权力制衡、制度稳定性解释为多中心结构生力网络的稳定态，提出最小结构角模型用于预测政体稳定性。  

**English**:  
Checks and balances and institutional stability are interpreted as stable states of a multi-center Structural Vital Force network, with the minimal structural angle model proposed to predict regime stability.

---

## 验证思路 (Validation Approach)

**中文**：  
对比不同国家制度结构与寿命：收集各国历史政体更替、权力分布数据，通过结构生力网络计算关键节点权重、最小结构角及临界阈值，分析制度稳定性与寿命的相关性。  

**English**:  
Compare institutional structures and lifespans across countries: collect historical regime transitions and power distribution data, use Structural Vital Force network to calculate key node weights, minimal structural angles, and critical thresholds, and analyze the correlation with regime stability and longevity.

---

## 理论公式示例 (Example Theoretical Formulas)

**中文**：  
政体稳定性方程：  
\[
\frac{dP_i}{dt} = \sum_j C_{ij} \cdot h(P_j, \theta_{ij}) - \epsilon_i P_i
\]  
其中 \(P_i\) 为政体节点权力能量，\(C_{ij}\) 为制度耦合强度，\(\theta_{ij}\) 为结构生力角度，\(\epsilon_i\) 为能量耗散系数。  

最小结构角条件：  
\[
\theta_{min} = \arg \min_{\theta_{ij}} \sum_{i,j} h(P_i, \theta_{ij}) 
\]  
保持 \(\theta_{min}\) 可使政体网络达到稳定平衡。  

**English**:  
Regime stability equation:  
\[
\frac{dP_i}{dt} = \sum_j C_{ij} \cdot h(P_j, \theta_{ij}) - \epsilon_i P_i
\]  
where \(P_i\) is node power energy, \(C_{ij}\) is institutional coupling strength, \(\theta_{ij}\) is Structural Vital Force angle, and \(\epsilon_i\) is energy dissipation coefficient.  

Minimal structural angle condition:  
\[
\theta_{min} = \arg \min_{\theta_{ij}} \sum_{i,j} h(P_i, \theta_{ij}) 
\]  
Maintaining \(\theta_{min}\) enables the regime network to achieve stable equilibrium.

## 研究展望 (Research Outlook)

**中文**：  
- 将结构生力模型用于现代多极政体稳定性分析与预测；  
- 探索权力调节、关键节点干预对政体韧性的影响；  
- 推动政治学、制度设计与复杂系统科学的跨学科研究。  

**English**:  
- Apply Structural Vital Force models to analyze and predict stability in modern multi-polar regimes.  
- Explore how power adjustment and key node interventions affect regime resilience.  
- Promote interdisciplinary research integrating political science, institutional design, and complex systems science.
